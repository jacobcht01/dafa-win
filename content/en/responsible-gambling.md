# Responsible Gambling India — Help, Tools & Resources

*Last updated: June 2025 | Author: Rahul Sharma, iGaming India Expert*

Gambling is entertainment — and for the vast majority of people who bet, it stays that way. But for some, it can become a problem. This page provides practical tools to keep gambling fun, and honest resources for anyone who needs support.

**18+ only. If you are under 18, do not gamble.**

---

## What Is Responsible Gambling?

Responsible gambling means betting within your means, staying in control of your time and money, and knowing when to stop. Key principles:

- **Set a budget** before you start — only gamble with money you can afford to lose
- **Set a time limit** — decide in advance how long you'll play
- **Treat it as entertainment** — not a way to make money or recover losses
- **Never chase losses** — this is the most common path to problem gambling
- **Take breaks** — step away regularly, especially during long sessions
- **Keep gambling separate from emotions** — don't bet when stressed, angry, or upset

## Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

Gambling can become a problem if you notice any of these signs in yourself or someone you know:

- Spending more money on gambling than intended
- Borrowing money to gamble
- Lying to family or friends about gambling habits
- Feeling anxious, irritable, or depressed when not gambling
- Gambling to escape from problems or negative feelings
- Neglecting work, family, or responsibilities because of gambling
- Failed attempts to cut down or stop gambling
- Chasing losses — trying to win back money you've lost

Recognising these signs early is the first step to getting help.

## Where to Get Help in India

If gambling is causing you distress, these organisations can help — all services are free and confidential:

### Vandrevala Foundation
**Helpline: 1860-2662-345**
Available 24/7, free of charge. Provides mental health support in multiple Indian languages including Hindi and Telugu. Trained counsellors offer immediate support and can help connect you with long-term treatment options.

### iCall (TISS)
**Helpline: 9152987821**
Run by the Tata Institute of Social Sciences. Free counselling available Monday–Saturday, 8am–10pm. Email support also available at icall@tiss.edu. Operates in English, Hindi, and other regional languages.

### Snehi Foundation
**Helpline: 044-24640050**
Chennai-based mental health helpline. Available daily 8am–10pm.

### National Health Helpline
**Helpline: 104**
Government mental health and general health helpline. Free to call from any mobile or landline.
